United Nations Security Council (UNSC)

The United Nations Security Council (UNSC) is one of the six principal organs of the United Nations (UN) and is charged with ensuring international peace and security.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Maintaining international peace and security
  • Recommending the admission of new UN members
  • Approving any changes to the UN Charter
  • Establishing peacekeeping operations

For more information about the UNSC and its current agenda, please contact our committee chairs.
